# Quality Standard

A course is ready only when all checks below pass.

The course has one sentence that states what the audience will be able to understand or do after the talk. It has no more than three takeaways. The opening establishes relevance within three minutes. Every takeaway is supported by a real case, a real demo, or verifiable evidence supplied in the source material. Generated hypothetical examples may be labeled as demonstrations, but they do not count as evidence.

The talk plan fills 30 to 60 minutes without exceeding the selected duration. Q&A receives a separate 5 to 10 minutes and cannot be used as overflow. Transitions make it clear why one section follows another. The ending returns to the one-sentence promise and the takeaways.

A cold audience reader must be able to restate the promise and at least two takeaways in their own words. Repeating the title is not sufficient. Undefined jargon, missing logical steps, parallel themes, unsupported absolutes, and an overloaded schedule are blockers.

The speaker-facing synthesis contains at most three blocking changes. All evidence requests and speaker actions are included inside those same blockers. It has no separate evidence backlog or follow-up checklist. Each change includes a directly usable correction. The speaker never has to reconcile multiple raw role reports.

The acceptance reviewer receives a clean context. It must not see previous approvals, raw role reports, coordinator reasoning, or pressure created by the event date. Missing evidence, unreadable material, or an invalid package produces `NEEDS_REVISION`.
