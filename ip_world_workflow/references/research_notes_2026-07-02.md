# Research Notes 2026-07-02

This note records the external references and Fandom-driven findings used to tighten the Studio World workflow.

## Large-IP coverage guidance

The current planning guidance comes from Fandom category-tree inspection plus selected reference pages rather than naive root-category counts.

- Star Wars should be treated as a true two-hundred-plus character world. Wookieepedia-linked reference material such as `Star Wars: Character Encyclopedia, Updated and Expanded Edition` explicitly frames the canon cast at more than 275 meaningful figures.
- The Lord of the Rings should be treated as a medium-to-large character world. Fandom major and minor character groupings plus film-trilogy character lists support planning in the dozens-to-low-hundreds rather than a toy sample.
- Harry Potter should be treated as a hundred-scale world at minimum. Fandom category structure is heavily fragmented across allegiance, family, house, era and ability, so small root counts are not trustworthy.

The practical grouping trigger should stay tied to long-tail density. When a coherent cluster would otherwise add more than roughly 8 to 12 low-distinction cards in the same semantic band, grouping becomes appropriate as long as major figures stay independent.

- visible cards should be natural prose dossiers
- one-line hooks are valuable

- source URLs, category dumps, reference arrays and prompt language should stay backstage
